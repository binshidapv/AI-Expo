Call for Abstracts: AIENI 2026

First International Conference of the Emirates Identity and Citizenship Academy.

Organizer

Emirates Academy of Identity and Citizenship

Location

Abu Dhabi, UAE

The Date

30-31 March 2026

Theme

AI in Enhancing National Identity

AIENI welcomes proposals to present at its 2026 International Conference.

The International Conference on Artificial Intelligence in Enhancing National Identity (AIENI) invites professionals, researchers, academics, and public sector leaders to submit abstracts that explore how AI technologies can strengthen, preserve, and promote national identity. The theme, "The Role of Artificial Intelligence in Enhancing National Identity," uniquely positions this conference at the intersection of cutting-edge technology and cultural heritage preservation.

Conference Strands

When submitting your abstract, your abstract should fit into one of the following strands:

01

The Legislative and Governance Framework of Artificial Intelligence and the Preservation of National Identity

  • Strengthening the legislative and governance framework to protect personal and symbolic data and ensure compliance with federal standards for digital and cultural identity.
  • Reinforcing the cybersecurity system and the governance of smart technologies to safeguard national data and enhance digital sovereignty.
02

Artificial Intelligence in Preserving Emirati Heritage and Enhancing National Memory

  • Employing artificial intelligence as a strategic tool to preserve Emirati heritage and reinforce national memory.
  • Cognitive representation and 3D digitization of tangible Emirati heritage using extended reality and intelligent restoration technologies.
03

Smart Media and the Protection of National Identity in the Digital Space

  • Smart national media as a strategic tool to strengthen belonging and safeguard digital identity.
  • Analyzing digital threats and proactively detecting misleading content to maintain stability of the national image.
04

Smart Education and the Promotion of National Identity and Societal Values

  • Integrating artificial intelligence into national curricula to enhance awareness of Emirati identity and foster values of leadership and belonging.
  • Interactive learning environments that simulate national symbols using semantic analysis and cognitive personalization for learners.
05

National Innovation and the Knowledge Economy for Protecting and Strengthening National Identity

  • Digital governance to enhance the reliability of the national digital identity within innovation and creativity ecosystems.
  • Developing nationally built, cyber-secured AI applications to protect cultural data and support the knowledge economy.
06

International Cooperation and Strategic Partnerships in Artificial Intelligence and the Preservation of National Identity

  • Strengthening international partnerships to establish joint frameworks for safeguarding cultural identity and exchanging expertise in digital governance and artificial intelligence.
  • Establishing cross-border cooperation initiatives to develop innovative solutions that support the preservation of national heritage and enhance the global presence of the Emirati identity.

Abstract Instructions:

Language

All submitted abstracts must be written in English or Arabic language.

Submit your Proposal

  • Abstracts must be submitted electronically via the conference portal.
  • SUBMISSION DEADLINE: February 1, 2026
  • Authors may offer no more than two abstract submissions in which they appear as the lead author.
  • It is a requirement that presenters whose abstract has been accepted and who wish to present their research must register to attend the full conference.
  • The following information is needed for each presenter: First name and Family Name, Institution or Affiliation, Country, and Email
  • A well-written abstract typically has the following sections: Background/objective, Design and methods, Results and Conclusions.
  • Submit your abstract (maximum 300 words), choosing from the available presentation formats listed below (Oral, Poster, or Panel).
  • Selection of the paper will be published as a book
Types of Presentations

Abstracts may be submitted for the following types of presentations:

01

Oral Presentation (20 minutes)

This is the standard presentation format. In an oral presentation the presenter delivers their research to their audience verbally, often accompanied by a Word document or slideshow. Oral presenters need to send their Word presentation 6 days prior to the conference to shadim@dndr.ae

02

Digital Poster Presentation (15 minutes)

An illustrated presentation, to be on display during the conference on the conference platform. Poster sessions will include photo/infographic and video/documentary presentations.

03

Panel Presentations (45 minutes)

Panel presentations are conceived by a panel organizer who recruit speakers to present in a coordinated session on a specific topic. Proposals should include clear descriptions of the topic or theme.

Acceptance & Rejection

The reviewers will judge the abstracts according to the scientific value, relevance to the conference, suitability of methods to aims, conclusions confirmed by objective results, description of methods used, ethics, originality of work, standard of English and overall impression. After completion of the evaluation of submitted abstracts, an 'Accepted' or 'Rejected' notification will be sent to authors generally within two weeks. The acceptance of an abstract does not imply the provision of travel, accommodation or registration for the conference, nor any other cost incurred in the preparation or presentation of the abstract, or attendance at the conference.
